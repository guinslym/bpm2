Kiwi PyCon 2016 Location: Dunedin, New Zealand Conference: 9/10/11 September Sprints: 12/13 September

Kiwi PyCon is an annual conference aimed at promoting and educating people about the Python programming language.

The New Zealand Python User Group is proud to present the eighth national Python conference in New Zealand.

The conference starts on Friday the 9th, offering 3 days of tutorials, talks, and other events including social activities.

The conference is followed by two days of development sprints. This is a separate event designed to help people contribute to the Python language itself.

 

Conference

Kiwi PyCon is a community-focussed conference for the New Zealand python community. The conference is three days of talks, tutorials, and many other activities. Talks at the conference cater to a wide range of abilities and skill levels. Everyone from seasoned professionals to curious amateurs will find something of value here.

We have a strong code of conduct, and encourage people who haven’t had the chance to experience Kiwi PyCon to attend in 2016.

Sprints

Want to learn how to contribute to the development of the Python language itself?

For the first time ever, Kiwi PyCon will be followed by two days of development sprints for core Python. Attending the sprints is free of charge, although registration will be required. This will be a great opportunity to meet other experienced Python developers and work together in the same room.
